Community Schools Initiative

The Community Schools Initiative calls for public schools to act as neighborhood centers that improve access to programs and services for students, families, and neighbors.  This initiative is intended to combat the steep barriers that children face outside of school that can impede their educational achievement.  Community schools work with a full-time coordinator to identify the communityâ€™s most pressing needs including access to medical services, after-school programming, and job training.

 

The Stress Index is part of the Community Schools Initiative data review process, which helped provide context for understanding neighborhoods, and was used in conjunction with information from surveys and focus groups.  The Stress Index was presented with data about neighborhood resources, health,  and census information to each community school committee for their specific neighborhood.  MOE will continue to use the Stress Index to provide context to current community schools as well as schools that apply to the next cohort.

 

The first step of a designated community school is to conduct a needs assessment to discover the strengths of the school and neighborhood assets, and identify priority needs to address.  MOE utilized a variety of tools to gather feedback from the school community about what needs were most important.  In addition, MOE analyzed community datasets including information from the American Community Survey, Public Health Management Corporation's Household Health Survey, the Community Health Assessment, and this tool - the Stress Index.
